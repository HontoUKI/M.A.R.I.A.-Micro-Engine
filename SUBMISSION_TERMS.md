# Character submission terms

Plain-English terms for submitting a character pack to be hosted in this
project's **approved** gallery. This is not legal advice, and the project
maintainer has final discretion over what is hosted.

By submitting a character pack (via issue, fork, or any other channel) for
inclusion, you confirm and agree to the following.

## 1. You have the rights

- You **own or have the necessary rights** to everything in the pack — the
  text you wrote and, critically, any **sprite art** it includes.
- The pack does **not** knowingly infringe anyone's copyright, trademark, or
  other rights.
- If a pack depicts a character from an existing work (a "fan work"), **you**
  are responsible for that use. Bundled art is the most common problem: do not
  submit ripped or unlicensed images. Text-only fan packs are lower risk but
  are still your responsibility.

## 2. You grant a hosting license

You grant the project a **non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, revocable**
license to store, display, reproduce, and distribute the submitted pack as part
of the gallery and its documentation. **You keep ownership** of your pack and
may share it anywhere else however you like.

## 3. No obligation, and takedown

- The maintainer is under **no obligation** to host, keep hosting, or approve
  any pack, and may remove any pack at any time for any reason.
- **Rights holders:** if you believe a hosted pack infringes your rights,
  open an issue titled `TAKEDOWN` (or use the contact link in the issue
  chooser) identifying the pack and the rights at stake. Good-faith requests
  are honored by removing the pack promptly, pending resolution.

## 4. Safety

Packs are prompt data executed by a language model. Submissions that steer
toward sexual content involving minors, targeted harassment, or other clearly
harmful output are refused and may be reported.

## 5. License of approved packs

Unless a pack states otherwise, an accepted pack is hosted under the license
its author declares in `pack.yaml` (`meta.license`). That license must permit
the project to redistribute the pack as part of the gallery. If it does not,
the pack cannot be accepted into the approved tier (you may still share it as
an unverified community pack via a fork).
